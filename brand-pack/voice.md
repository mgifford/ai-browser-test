# Voice and Tone

**Audience**: Developers, QA Engineers, and Browser Vendors.

### Tone by Context
- **Implementation Docs**: Direct, imperative, and technical.
- **Status/Errors**: Transparent and helpful (don't say "Error", say "WebGPU not detected").
- **Marketing/README**: Enthusiastic but evidence-based.

### Spelling & Grammar
- **Convention**: US English (e.g., "color", "initialize").
- **Case**: Sentence case for headings (e.g., "Browser support table", not "Browser Support Table").

### Word Substitutions
- **Avoid**: "Easy", "Simple", "Just" (diminishes developer effort).
- **Use**: "Efficient", "Direct", "Automated".
- **Browser AI**: Refer to "On-device AI" or "Built-in AI" to distinguish from Cloud APIs.
